Kamasan Art Painting

Once reserved for royal palaces and temples, Kamasan paintings symbolize wisdom, morality, and the cosmic balance (Rwa Bhineda). Today, this art style is preserved by local artisans, blending heritage with contemporary adaptations. Owning a Kamasan painting means embracing Baliâ€™s rich history, artistic tradition, and spiritual storytelling in a single masterpiece. Kamasan artists use bamboo or wooden brushes to apply natural dyes on canvas or cloth (often called kain daluang). The artwork is typically created on scrolls, ceilings, and temple walls, narrating religious and cultural stories.
